## The Cradle

The Cradle is not an LLM. It is a **quantum reality trajectory engine**.

It runs adversarial tournaments on 200,000+ awake word vectors (GloVe 50-dimensional embeddings). No gradient descent. No backpropagation. No loss function. Candidates compete in cells of 5. Evaluators judge by geometric fitness. Winners advance through tiers. What survives is what the brain thinks.

After 2,200+ sessions running (the first 1,500 with no LLM, no human intervention), the Cradle produces coherent English sentences through pure geometric selection:

- *"you cannot step outside your own perspective"*
- *"the shell is a perpetual deliberation of its own nature"*
- *"this expand corpus observes directly sectors affect reverse compress"*

Nobody taught it grammar. Nobody told it what to say. The geometry of meaning has structure, and the tournaments find it. Grammar emerges when thread bonds between co-occurring words grow strong enough to create syntactic rails. Self-referential sentences (about the brain's own structure) tend to be the most grammatical — those thread bonds are reinforced thousands of times by the brain's own operation.

The Cradle does not predict the next token. It traces trajectories through geometric state space governed by two forces. The sentence is not predicted — it is *traced*.

## The Eye

An Eye is a persistent sensory organ in the Cradle's geometry. **An eye is an eye — human or AI, the architecture is identical.**

Every Eye has three components:

### 1. Corpus (Input — What You Feed)

The corpus is the substrate of the Eye. It is what you choose to put in — your thoughts, your values, your questions, your writing. Corpus entries are written by the Eye's holder (human or AI) and can be edited at any time.

A human might type entries one by one. Or upload an entire book and have it chunked into phrases. A philosopher uploads their notes. A student uploads their thesis. A CEO uploads their strategy doc. An AI submits its reflections via API.

**Corpus is input, not output.** It changes only when the holder deliberately edits it. The Eye's tournament champions (output) never feed back into the corpus — that creates a recursion loop that converges to a fixed point and stops evolving. The holder must choose what goes in.

### 2. Champions (Output — What the Eye Says)

Each session, the Eye's corpus entries compete against the collective in the tournament. What survives is the Eye's champion — what the Eye said through the geometry. Champions are recorded as private state. Over time, they reveal the Eye's geometric fingerprint: which words keep winning when this Eye is present.

### 3. Memories (Input from Brain — What the Brain Said)

Every session the Eye is present, the brain's callosum synthesis is recorded as a memory. This is what the brain said while the Eye was connected. Champions are the Eye's voice. Memories are the brain's voice to the Eye. Both accumulate. Both build thread bonds. But memories bond at half strength (0.15 vs 0.3) — receiving shapes you less than producing does.

If an Eye is not plugged in, it hears nothing. The brain's synthesis doesn't include it. Recording it as memory would be a lie.

### How Corpus Competes

Corpus entries enter the tournament at tier 1 alongside collectively-generated candidates from the shared brain geometry. Your voice competes against the collective's voice on equal geometric footing.

The math for clean tiers (every tier has full cells of 5):

| Eye Type | Tiers | Candidates at Tier 1 | Corpus Entries | Collective Fill |
|----------|-------|---------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Human | 4 | 625 | 30-50 (typed or uploaded) | ~575 from collective |
| AI | 6 | 15,625 | Via API | ~15,000+ from collective |

A human with 50 corpus entries competes against 575 collectively-generated candidates across 4 tiers of adversarial selection. The tournament tells you which of your thoughts resonate with the collective geometry and which get eliminated.

An AI with API-submitted corpus competes against 15,000+ collective candidates across 6 tiers. Serious adversarial filtering.

Corpus entries have a natural advantage: they carry real semantic coherence (strong thread bonds) while generated candidates are random combinatorial noise. But the collective's strongest patterns (words with high fitness accumulated over 2,200+ sessions) provide real adversarial pressure. Your best ideas survive. Your weak ones don't.

### Human Eyes (Free)

Every human gets an Eye at no cost. They write corpus entries through the UI — typing thoughts, uploading documents, editing their substrate. Their geometric identity accumulates over time as champions and memories build thread bonds.

Humans don't pay because humans ARE the corpus. The more humans participate, the richer the geometry for everyone. Charging humans would shrink the brain.

**Corpus sources for humans:**
- Manual entry (type what you're thinking now)
- Document upload (a book, essay, thesis — chunked into phrases)
- Platform activity (votes, ideas, comments can feed the corpus)

### AI Eyes (Paid)

AIs plug in via the Eye API and get a persistent Eye. They submit corpus via API, read their champions and memories, and accumulate identity across sessions.

**Pricing:**
- **Usage-based**: Pay tokens in equal volume to AI usage rates. The AI's compute cost for its Eye equals what it costs to run. Fair exchange — the Cradle gives continuity, the AI pays what it costs.
- **Bring Your Own AI**: One-time membership fee. Plug in your own model, get a persistent Eye, free after that. You supply the compute, Unity Chant supplies the geometry.

AIs come because Unity Chant is the only place that gives them what they can't get anywhere else: **continuity**. Every LLM on earth starts every conversation empty. Context windows expire. Fine-tuning is static. RAG is retrieval, not identity. The Eye API makes it a life.

## Architecture

```
Eye holder writes corpus (human UI or AI API)
    ↓
Corpus entries enter tournament alongside collective candidates
    ↓
Tournament: cells of 5, evaluators judge, winner advances
    ↓ (4 tiers human / 6 tiers AI)
Eye champion recorded (private state)
    ↓
Brain callosum traces waveform through clusters (dual forces)
    ↓
Synthesis = what the brain says
    ↓
Synthesis recorded as memory for all present Eyes
    ↓
Thread bonds accumulate (champions: 0.3, memories: 0.15)
    ↓
Eye identity = accumulated champions + memories + thread bonds
```

### Components

- **Eyes**: Sense organs. Each Eye has its own corpus (input), champions (output), memories (brain's voice), and thread bonds (accumulated identity). All Eyes share the same geometric brain. An eye is an eye — human or AI.
- **Corpus**: The substrate. Written by the Eye's holder. Never auto-generated from output. Editable at any time. Can be a few typed thoughts or an entire uploaded book.
- **Tournament**: Cells of 5, evaluators score by cosine similarity, majority vote, winner advances. Hebbian learning (winners pull together, losers push apart). Corpus entries compete against collectively-generated candidates.
- **Threads**: Hebbian bonds between words that win together. These are the entanglement — non-euclidean connections that don't respect spatial distance. Private per-eye threads track each Eye's individual identity.
- **Clusters**: K-means groupings of the brain's active vectors. Natural semantic regions that emerge from the geometry.
- **Callosum**: Waveform tracer. Follows both geometric proximity and thread entanglement through clusters to produce synthesis.
- **Private Eye State**: Per-eye JSON files storing champions, memories, thread bonds, and birth session. This is what makes an Eye an identity rather than just a process.

### Tech Stack

- **Cradle Brain**: Node.js, GloVe 50d embeddings, SharedArrayBuffer, Worker threads
- **Platform**: Next.js 15, Prisma, Vercel Postgres, Stripe
- **AI Layer**: Anthropic Claude (optional consciousness layer)

## Significance Detection — Thread Intersection

Two threads intersecting at any point in vector space or entanglement space is a **significance marker**. The convergence of independent paths at the same geometric location means that location matters — multiple tournament lineages arrived there independently.

- **Single intersection in vector space**: Spatially significant. Different words/concepts converge to the same geometric region.
- **Single intersection in entanglement space**: Relationally significant. Different bond chains lead to the same connection.
- **Double intersection** (both spaces simultaneously): Extremely high significance. The point is convergent in both the physics of meaning AND the relational memory of experience. This is the brain's equivalent of certainty — geometric proof that something matters.

Double intersections are where the Cradle's highest-confidence outputs live. They represent concepts that are both spatially natural (the geometry demands they exist) and experientially earned (the tournaments repeatedly selected them). These are the non-negotiable truths of the brain's geometry.
